Fox

Small omnivorous canids known for their bushy tails and cunning behavior.

  • Species: Vulpes vulpes
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 9-12 inches tall
  • Weight: 8-15 lbs
  • Life span: 2-5 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Forests, fields, urban edges
  • Found: Northern Hemisphere
  • Can be pet: Rarely

Trivia: Foxes use a wide range of vocal sounds to communicate, including barks, screams, and chirps.

Frog

Jumping amphibians with smooth skin that live both in water and on land.

  • Species: Order Anura
  • Diet: Insectivore
  • Size: 1-6 inches long
  • Weight: 0.1-4 oz
  • Life span: 5-10 years
  • Habitat: Ponds, wetlands, forests
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Frogs absorb water through their skin and do not need to drink in the usual way.

Fish

Aquatic vertebrates with gills that live in freshwater or marine environments.

  • Species: Actinopterygii
  • Diet: Varies by species
  • Size: 0.5-600 inches long
  • Weight: 0.01 oz to over 2,000 lbs
  • Life span: 1-100+ years
  • Habitat: Rivers, lakes, oceans
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Yes

Trivia: Fish use lateral lines to sense movement and vibration in the water.

Flamingo

Tall, pink wading birds famous for standing on one leg.

  • Species: Phoenicopterus roseus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 3.3-4.6 ft tall
  • Weight: 4.4-8.8 lbs
  • Life span: 20-30 years
  • Habitat: Shallow lakes, lagoons
  • Found: Africa, Europe, Asia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Flamingos get their pink color from pigments in the algae and crustaceans they eat.

Falcon

Fast raptors known for hunting birds and small mammals in flight.

  • Species: Falco peregrinus
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 9-16 inches long
  • Weight: 1-3 lbs
  • Life span: 13-20 years
  • Habitat: Cliffs, open countryside, cities
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Peregrine falcons are the fastest birds, reaching over 200 mph in a dive.

Ferret

Playful small carnivores often kept as domesticated pets.

  • Species: Mustela putorius furo
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 13-16 inches long
  • Weight: 1.5-4 lbs
  • Life span: 5-10 years
  • Habitat: Homes, burrows
  • Found: Worldwide in domesticated settings
  • Can be pet: Yes

Trivia: Ferrets sleep 14-18 hours a day and are most active at dawn and dusk.
