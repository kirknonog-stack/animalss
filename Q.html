Quail

Small ground-dwelling birds with short wings and plump bodies.

  • Species: Coturnix coturnix
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 6-8 inches long
  • Weight: 2-6 ounces
  • Life span: 2-5 years
  • Habitat: Grasslands, farmlands
  • Found: Europe, Asia, Africa
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Quail are often kept for their eggs and can fly short distances.

Quokka

Small marsupials known for their friendly expression.

  • Species: Setonix brachyurus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 16-26 inches long
  • Weight: 4-11 lbs
  • Life span: 10 years
  • Habitat: Shrublands, forests
  • Found: Rottnest Island, Australia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Quokkas are nicknamed the happiest animals because of their smiling faces.

Quoll

Carnivorous marsupials native to Australia and New Guinea.

  • Species: Dasyurus
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 14-25 inches long
  • Weight: 1-10 lbs
  • Life span: 5-7 years
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands
  • Found: Australia, New Guinea
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Quolls are nocturnal hunters with sharp teeth and a keen sense of smell.

Quetzal

Colorful forest birds prized for their long tail feathers.

  • Species: Pharomachrus mocinno
  • Diet: Frugivore
  • Size: 11-13 inches long
  • Weight: 7-9 ounces
  • Life span: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Cloud forests
  • Found: Central America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: The resplendent quetzal was sacred to ancient Mesoamerican civilizations.

Queen Snake

A slender, nonvenomous snake commonly found near streams.

  • Species: Regina septemvittata
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 22-38 inches long
  • Weight: 4-8 ounces
  • Life span: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Freshwater streams, wetlands
  • Found: Eastern North America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Queen snakes feed mainly on freshwater crayfish and their larvae.

Quahog

A hard-shelled edible clam found in Atlantic saltwater beds.

  • Species: Mercenaria mercenaria
  • Diet: Filter feeder
  • Size: 3-4 inches across
  • Weight: 1-2 ounces
  • Life span: 40-100 years
  • Habitat: Estuaries, sandy bottoms
  • Found: Atlantic coast of North America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Quahogs can live for decades and are used in clam chowder and sushi.
