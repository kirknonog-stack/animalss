X-ray Fish

Small transparent fish known for their ghostly appearance.

  • Species: Prionobrama polylepis
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 1.5-2 inches long
  • Weight: Few ounces
  • Life span: 3-5 years
  • Habitat: Freshwater streams
  • Found: South America
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: X-ray fish are prized in aquariums for their see-through bodies.

Xenops

Tiny woodcreeping birds found in tropical forests.

  • Species: Xenops
  • Diet: Insectivore
  • Size: 4-5 inches long
  • Weight: 0.3-0.4 ounces
  • Life span: 5-7 years
  • Habitat: Forest canopies
  • Found: Central and South America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Xenops use their curved bills to probe bark for insects.

Xerus

African ground squirrels that live in open savannas.

  • Species: Xerus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 8-14 inches long
  • Weight: 1-2 lbs
  • Life span: 5-7 years
  • Habitat: Grasslands, scrublands
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Xerus often dig burrows and forage for seeds and roots.

Xantus Hummingbird

A tiny hummingbird with metallic green plumage.

  • Species: Basilinna xantusii
  • Diet: Nectarivore
  • Size: 4-4.5 inches long
  • Weight: 0.2-0.3 ounces
  • Life span: 5-7 years
  • Habitat: Shrublands, forests
  • Found: Baja California
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Xantus Hummingbirds hover and feed on flower nectar.

Xenarthra

A group that includes anteaters, sloths, and armadillos.

  • Species: Xenarthra
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: Varies widely
  • Weight: Varies widely
  • Life span: 5-20 years
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands
  • Found: Americas
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Xenarthrans are named for the extra joints in their spinal columns.

Xeme

A seabird also called the Sabine's gull, found over oceans.

  • Species: Xema sabini
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 14-16 inches long
  • Weight: 6-8 ounces
  • Life span: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Arctic seas
  • Found: Northern oceans
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Xemes migrate long distances each year between Arctic and temperate zones.
