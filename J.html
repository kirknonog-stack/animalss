Jaguar

Powerful big cats native to the Americas, known for strong jaws.

  • Species: Panthera onca
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 2-3 ft tall
  • Weight: 100-250 lbs
  • Life span: 12-15 years
  • Habitat: Rainforests, wetlands
  • Found: Central and South America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Jaguars can bite through turtle shells and skulls thanks to their strong bite force.

Jellyfish

Gelatinous marine animals that drift through oceans and sting prey.

  • Species: Scyphozoa
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 0.5 in to 6 ft diameter
  • Weight: Very light
  • Life span: Weeks to months
  • Habitat: Oceans
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Some jellyfish are bioluminescent and glow in the dark.

Jackal

Medium-sized wild canines that scavenge and hunt in pairs or small groups.

  • Species: Canis aureus
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 15-20 inches tall
  • Weight: 15-30 lbs
  • Life span: 8-10 years
  • Habitat: Grasslands, savannas
  • Found: Africa, Asia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Jackals often howl to communicate across long distances.

Jay

Colorful songbirds known for loud calls and intelligent behavior.

  • Species: Corvus monedula
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 9-12 inches long
  • Weight: 2-4 oz
  • Life span: 7-10 years
  • Habitat: Woodlands, parks
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Jays can mimic the calls of other birds and even some human sounds.

Jerboa

Small desert rodents with long hind legs for hopping across sand.

  • Species: Dipodidae
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 2-4 inches long
  • Weight: 1-3 oz
  • Life span: 2-5 years
  • Habitat: Deserts
  • Found: Asia, Northern Africa
  • Can be pet: Rarely

Trivia: Jerboas can leap up to 6 feet in a single jump.

Japanese Macaque

Also called snow monkeys, these primates live in cold mountain forests.

  • Species: Macaca fuscata
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 20-24 inches tall
  • Weight: 10-20 lbs
  • Life span: 20-30 years
  • Habitat: Forests, mountains
  • Found: Japan
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Japanese macaques soak in hot springs to stay warm in winter.
