Goat

Domesticated and wild hoofed mammals raised for milk, meat, and fiber.

  • Species: Capra aegagrus hircus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 2-3 ft tall
  • Weight: 45-300 lbs
  • Life span: 15-18 years
  • Habitat: Farms, hillsides, scrubland
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Goats have excellent memories and can learn the names of other goats and humans.

Giraffe

The tallest land mammals, known for their long necks and spotted coats.

  • Species: Giraffa camelopardalis
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 14-19 ft tall
  • Weight: 1,600-3,000 lbs
  • Life span: 20-25 years
  • Habitat: Savannas, open woodlands
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: A giraffe's tongue can be up to 20 inches long and is darkly pigmented to protect against sunburn.

Gorilla

Large, intelligent primates that live in family groups in tropical forests.

  • Species: Gorilla beringei
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 4-5.5 ft tall
  • Weight: 300-450 lbs
  • Life span: 35-40 years
  • Habitat: Mountain and lowland forests
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Gorillas share about 98% of their DNA with humans.

Goose

Waterfowl commonly found near lakes and ponds with loud, honking calls.

  • Species: Anser anser
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 2-4 ft wingspan
  • Weight: 5-14 lbs
  • Life span: 10-20 years
  • Habitat: Wetlands, fields
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Geese mate for life and often return to the same nesting site each year.

Goldfish

Popular aquarium fish that come in many colors and varieties.

  • Species: Carassius auratus
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 2-12 inches long
  • Weight: 0.2-3 lbs
  • Life span: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Aquariums, ponds
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Yes

Trivia: Goldfish can recognize faces and learn simple tricks.

Gecko

Small lizards known for climbing walls and making chirping sounds.

  • Species: Gekkonidae
  • Diet: Insectivore
  • Size: 3-10 inches long
  • Weight: 0.1-4 oz
  • Life span: 5-10 years
  • Habitat: Forests, homes, rocky areas
  • Found: Worldwide in warm climates
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Geckos can detach their tails to escape predators and later regrow them.
