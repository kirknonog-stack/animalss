Tiger

Powerful striped big cats native to Asia.

  • Species: Panthera tigris
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 3-3.5 ft tall
  • Weight: 220-660 lbs
  • Life span: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands
  • Found: Asia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Tigers are the largest cat species in the world.

Turtle

Reptiles with a hard shell that live in water and on land.

  • Species: Testudines
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 4-40 inches long
  • Weight: 1-1,000 lbs
  • Life span: 20-100 years
  • Habitat: Oceans, lakes, forests
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Turtles can withdraw into their shells for protection.

Turkey

Large game birds often raised for their meat.

  • Species: Meleagris gallopavo
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 3-4 ft long
  • Weight: 10-25 lbs
  • Life span: 10 years
  • Habitat: Woods, farms
  • Found: North America
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Turkeys can run up to 20 mph and fly short distances.

Toad

Stocky amphibians with dry, bumpy skin.

  • Species: Anura
  • Diet: Insectivore
  • Size: 2-6 inches long
  • Weight: 0.5-4 ounces
  • Life span: 5-10 years
  • Habitat: Gardens, wetlands
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Toads can absorb water through their skin instead of drinking.

Tapir

Large browsing mammals with a short, trunk-like snout.

  • Species: Tapirus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 2.5-3.5 ft tall
  • Weight: 300-700 lbs
  • Life span: 25-30 years
  • Habitat: Forests, swamps
  • Found: Americas, Asia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Tapirs are excellent swimmers and often live near water.

Tarantula

Large hairy spiders often found in tropical regions.

  • Species: Theraphosidae
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 4-12 inches legspan
  • Weight: 1-3 ounces
  • Life span: 10-30 years
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands
  • Found: Americas, Africa, Asia
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Tarantulas flick hairs from their abdomen to defend themselves.
