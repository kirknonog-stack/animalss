Bat

The only mammals capable of sustained flight.

  • Species: Chiroptera
  • Diet: Insectivore or frugivore
  • Size: 3-20 inches wingspan
  • Weight: 0.5-3 lbs
  • Life span: 10-30 years
  • Habitat: Caves, forests, buildings
  • Found: Worldwide except Antarctica
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Some bats can eat up to 1,000 insects in one hour.

Beaver

Engineers of streams, famous for building dams and lodges.

  • Species: Castor canadensis
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 3-4 ft
  • Weight: 30-70 lbs
  • Life span: 10-20 years
  • Habitat: Freshwater rivers, ponds
  • Found: North America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: A beaver's front teeth never stop growing, so they must gnaw constantly.

Bison

Massive grazers of North American plains and grasslands.

  • Species: Bison bison
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 6-11 ft
  • Weight: 900-2,200 lbs
  • Life span: 15-20 years
  • Habitat: Prairies, grasslands
  • Found: North America
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Bison can sprint up to 35 mph despite their size.

Buffalo

Strong bovines often found in African savannas and Asian wetlands.

  • Species: Syncerus caffer
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 5-6.5 ft at shoulder
  • Weight: 1,100-2,200 lbs
  • Life span: 18-25 years
  • Habitat: Grasslands, floodplains, forests
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Buffalo can hold their breath for up to six minutes when submerged.

Baboon

Social primates that live in large troops across Africa.

  • Species: Papio anubis
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 20-34 inches
  • Weight: 33-82 lbs
  • Life span: 20-30 years
  • Habitat: Savannas, woodlands, hills
  • Found: Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Male baboons have powerful canine teeth used for dominance displays.
