Monkey

Social primates that live in troops across forests.

  • Species: Various primates
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 12-24 inches tall
  • Weight: 8-30 lbs
  • Life span: 15-30 years
  • Habitat: Tropical forests
  • Found: Africa, Asia, Americas
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Many monkeys use their tails for balance and swinging through trees.

Mouse

Small rodents known for quick movements and large ears.

  • Species: Mus musculus
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 2-4 inches long
  • Weight: 0.5-1 ounce
  • Life span: 1-3 years
  • Habitat: Fields, homes, forests
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Yes

Trivia: Mice have excellent night vision and can squeeze through very small gaps.

Moose

The largest members of the deer family with broad antlers.

  • Species: Alces alces
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 6-7 ft tall
  • Weight: 600-1,600 lbs
  • Life span: 15-20 years
  • Habitat: Boreal forests, wetlands
  • Found: North America, Europe, Asia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Moose can swim long distances and often feed on aquatic plants.

Meerkat

Small desert mammals that stand guard in burrows.

  • Species: Suricata suricatta
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 9-11 inches tall
  • Weight: 1-2 lbs
  • Life span: 6-10 years
  • Habitat: Deserts, grasslands
  • Found: Southern Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Meerkats take turns watching for predators while others forage.

Mule

A hybrid between a horse and a donkey, prized for endurance.

  • Species: Hybrid
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 5-6 ft tall
  • Weight: 800-1,000 lbs
  • Life span: 30-40 years
  • Habitat: Farms, ranches
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Mules inherit strength and sure-footedness from both parents.

Manatee

Gentle sea cows that graze on underwater plants.

  • Species: Trichechus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 8-13 ft long
  • Weight: 800-1,200 lbs
  • Life span: 40-60 years
  • Habitat: Shallow coastal waters
  • Found: Americas, Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Manatees are also called sea cows because they eat mostly seagrass.
