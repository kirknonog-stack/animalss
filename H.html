Horse

Large domesticated mammals used for riding, racing, and farm work.

  • Species: Equus ferus caballus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 4.5-6 ft tall
  • Weight: 900-2,200 lbs
  • Life span: 25-30 years
  • Habitat: Stables, pastures
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Yes

Trivia: Horses can sleep standing up thanks to a specialized stay apparatus in their legs.

Hippo

Large, semi-aquatic mammals that spend much of their time in rivers and lakes.

  • Species: Hippopotamus amphibius
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 4.5-5 ft tall
  • Weight: 3,000-4,000 lbs
  • Life span: 40-50 years
  • Habitat: Rivers, lakes
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Hippos secrete a natural red-colored substance that acts like sunscreen and antibacterial lotion.

Hyena

Social carnivores best known for their loud laughter-like calls.

  • Species: Crocuta crocuta
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 2.5 ft tall
  • Weight: 90-190 lbs
  • Life span: 12-25 years
  • Habitat: Savannas, grasslands
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Hyenas have one of the strongest bites in the animal kingdom, capable of crushing bone.

Hamster

Small rodents often kept as friendly and easy-to-care-for pets.

  • Species: Cricetinae
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 2-6 inches long
  • Weight: 0.5-5 oz
  • Life span: 2-3 years
  • Habitat: Grasslands, fields
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Yes

Trivia: Hamsters store food in expandable cheek pouches and can carry it back to their burrows.

Hedgehog

Small spiny mammals that curl into a ball when threatened.

  • Species: Erinaceinae
  • Diet: Insectivore
  • Size: 5-12 inches long
  • Weight: 0.5-2 lbs
  • Life span: 3-7 years
  • Habitat: Meadows, woodlands, gardens
  • Found: Europe, Africa, Asia
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Hedgehogs are mostly nocturnal and use their spines for defense.

Hawk

Birds of prey that hunt small animals using excellent vision and flight.

  • Species: Buteo jamaicensis
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 18-26 inches long
  • Weight: 1-4 lbs
  • Life span: 10-20 years
  • Habitat: Forests, fields, wetlands
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Hawks can see up to eight times more clearly than humans.
