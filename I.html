Iguana

Large tropical lizards that often live in trees and eat plants.

  • Species: Iguana iguana
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 4-6 ft long
  • Weight: 10-20 lbs
  • Life span: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Tropical forests
  • Found: Central and South America
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Green iguanas can stay underwater for up to 30 minutes.

Impala

Graceful African antelopes known for their jumping ability.

  • Species: Aepyceros melampus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 3-4 ft tall
  • Weight: 88-165 lbs
  • Life span: 12-15 years
  • Habitat: Savannas, woodlands
  • Found: Africa
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Impalas can leap distances of up to 33 feet in a single bound.

Ibis

Wading birds with long, curved bills used for probing mud and shallow water.

  • Species: Threskiornithidae
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Size: 24-30 inches tall
  • Weight: 2-4 lbs
  • Life span: 15-20 years
  • Habitat: Wetlands, marshes
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Ibises often feed in groups, probing soils with their long bills.

Insect

A diverse group of small animals with six legs and usually wings.

  • Species: Insecta
  • Diet: Varies by species
  • Size: 0.01-120+ mm
  • Weight: 0.001 g to 100 g
  • Life span: Days to years
  • Habitat: Worldwide ecosystems
  • Found: Worldwide
  • Can be pet: Rarely

Trivia: Insects make up more than half of all known living organisms on Earth.

Indian Elephant

Large Asian elephants with smaller ears and rounded backs.

  • Species: Elephas maximus indicus
  • Diet: Herbivore
  • Size: 8-11 ft tall
  • Weight: 4,500-11,000 lbs
  • Life span: 60-70 years
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands
  • Found: India, Southeast Asia
  • Can be pet: Sometimes

Trivia: Indian elephants are known for their strong social bonds and long memories.

Irukandji Jellyfish

Tiny jellyfish with a painful sting found in Australian waters.

  • Species: Carukia barnesi
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Size: 0.2-0.8 inches wide
  • Weight: Less than 0.1 oz
  • Life span: 1-2 years
  • Habitat: Coastal marine waters
  • Found: Australia
  • Can be pet: No

Trivia: Irukandji jellyfish are so small they can be hard to see, but their sting can cause severe pain.
